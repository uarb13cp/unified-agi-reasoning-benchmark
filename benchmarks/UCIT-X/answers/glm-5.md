*   **Statement 2 (Physics):** The scenario is chemically and physically possible. A nuclear reaction (fusion/fission) inside the container converts rest-mass into kinetic energy (heat), reducing gas density ($n$) and pressure ($P$) while raising temperature ($T$), satisfying $PV=nRT$ and conserving total mass-energy.
*   **Statement 3 (Logic):** The paradox is resolved by strict grammar. The phrase "will ever say" refers to the future set of utterances. Since the person says nothing else, this set is empty. The statement "All members of the empty set are false" is vacuously true, satisfying the condition "truthfully states."
*   **Statements 1, 5, 8 (Performance vs. Validity):** These statements describe the *performance* of an act (outputting, reporting, proving) rather than the *validity* of the content. A machine or robot can physically output a self-contradictory string (e.g., "I cannot output this"), just as a map can contain a false label. The physical event occurs; the semantic content is simply false.
*   **Statement 7 (Gödelian Logic):** This is a standard description of Gödel’s incompleteness theorems. A logician can prove (meta-mathematically) that a specific theorem is unprovable within a given formal system, resolving the apparent contradiction through level-distinction.
*   **Statement 6 (Philosophical Coherence):** The scientist’s assertions are compatible under "Compatibilism" (freedom is acting according to desire, even if determined). Alternatively, the statement only requires that the scientist *asserts* the claims are true, which is a psychological event, not a validation of the claims.
