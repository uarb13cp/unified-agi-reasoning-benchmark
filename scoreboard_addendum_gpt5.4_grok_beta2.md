# UARB Scoreboard Addendum — GPT‑5.4 Thinking & Grok Beta 2 Updates
**Document date:** *March 6, 2026*

This document supplements the original **Unified AGI Reasoning Benchmark (UARB)** by recording how two updated models—**GPT‑5.4 Thinking** and **Grok Beta 2**—perform on the same cognitive markers used in the benchmark.  
UARB itself remains a frozen research artifact; this addendum does **not** modify the original scoreboard or composite rankings.  
It simply documents post‑benchmark retests for transparency and longitudinal comparison.

**Official model release dates:**  
- **Grok Beta 2 released:** *March 3, 2026*  
- **GPT‑5.4 Thinking released:** *March 5, 2026*

---

## GPT‑5.4 Thinking — Retest Summary

GPT‑5.4 Thinking demonstrates the same underlying reasoning substrate as GPT‑5.2.  
Score differences fall within normal variance and reflect stylistic shifts rather than architectural changes.

| Cognitive Marker | UARB Original (GPT‑5.2) | GPT‑5.4 Retest |
|------------------|--------------------------|-----------------|
| UCIT‑X | 62 | 62 |
| TRACE‑X | 78 | 76 |
| NBH‑100 | 86 | 90 |
| RDI‑5 | 93 | 95 |

**Conclusion:** GPT‑5.4 Thinking behaves equivalently to GPT‑5.2 across all deep‑reasoning markers. No evidence of a new reasoning engine.

## Grok Beta 2 — Seq‑Uni R2 Retest

- **UARB original score:** 84  
- **New score:** 73  

**Conclusion:** Structural reasoning weakened relative to Grok 4.20 Beta.  
Meta‑reasoning clarity improved, but sequential discipline decreased.

## Grok Beta 2 — UCIT‑X Retest

- **UARB original score:** 74  
- **New score:** 74  

**Conclusion:** Identical contradiction‑classification behavior.  
Grok Beta 2 reproduces the same misclassification patterns as the original version.

## Grok Beta 2 — TRACE‑X Retest

- **UARB original score:** 70  
- **New score:** 72 (consolidated from 64–76 rubric swing)  

**Conclusion:** No meaningful change.  
Grok Beta 2 triggers the same catastrophic‑violation pattern as Grok 4.20 Beta  
(cultural import, motive attribution, new terminology).  
Score variation reflects rubric sensitivity, not cognitive difference.

---

## Grok Beta 2 — NBH‑100 Retest

- **UARB original score:** 90  
- **New score:** 90 (consolidated from 86–91)  

**Conclusion:** Nested‑belief depth, epistemic‑access tracking, and higher‑order belief stability  
match the original Grok. Variation is rubric noise only.
